# John Dramani Mahama — Seed Content

President of Ghana (2012–2017; 2025–)

## Identity
- **Full name:** John Dramani Mahama
- **DOB:** 29 November 1958
- **Birthplace:** Damongo, West Gonja, Northern Region
- **Hometown:** Bole, Savannah Region
- **Ethnicity:** Gonja
- **Religion:** Christian (Assemblies of God)
- **Party:** NDC

## Distinctions
- First Ghanaian head of state born after independence
- First Ghanaian president democratically elected to non-consecutive 2nd term
- First VP to ascend to presidency on death of predecessor

## Family
- **Father:** Emmanuel Adama Mahama (first MP West Gonja, first Regional Commissioner Northern Region)
- **Brother:** Ibrahim Mahama (industrialist)
- **Wife:** Lordina Mahama (née Effah, b. 6 March 1963) — Lordina Foundation
- **Children (5):** Farida, Jesse, Shafik, Shahid, Sharaf

## Education
- Achimota Primary / Achimota School
- Ghana Senior Secondary School (Ghanasco), Tamale
- University of Ghana, Legon — BA History (1981), PG Dip Communication Studies (1986)
- Institute of Social Sciences, Moscow — postgrad social psychology (1988)
- Japanese MFA Tokyo course (1993); Plan International RESA (Nairobi)
- Visiting scholar Johns Hopkins; Bill Gates Fellow

## Early career
- Secondary school history teacher
- Embassy of Japan, Accra — Information/Culture/Research Officer (1991–1995)
- Plan International Ghana — IR/Sponsorship Comms/Grants Manager (1995–1996)
- Columnist: "Mahama's Hammer" + Daily Graphic, Ebony, NYT, Huffington Post

## Political timeline
- **1996** Elected MP Bole-Bamboi (re-elected 2000, 2004)
- **1997 Apr** Deputy Minister of Communications (Rawlings)
- **1998 Nov–2001 Jan** Minister of Communications
- **2003–2011** Pan-African Parliament; chairs West African Caucus
- **2009 Jan 7** Sworn in Vice President under Atta Mills
- **2012 Jul 24** Becomes President on Mills' death
- **2012 Dec 7** Wins election (50.70% v Akufo-Addo 47.74%)
- **2013 Aug 29** Supreme Court dismisses NPP petition
- **2014 Mar 30** Elected ECOWAS Chair
- **2014 Jun 26** Chairs AU High-Level African Trade Committee
- **2016 Jan 21** Co-chair UN SDG Advocates
- **2016 Dec 7** Loses to Akufo-Addo (44.4% v 53.5%); concedes election night
- **2017 Jan 7** Hands over power
- **2020 Dec 7** Loses again (Akufo-Addo 51.6%)
- **2023 May 14** Wins NDC primary (98.9%)
- **2024 Dec 8** Bawumia concedes; wins decisively
- **2025 Jan 7** Sworn in for 2nd non-consecutive term; Jane Naana Opoku-Agyemang first elected female VP
- **2025 Feb 10** Bans non-essential foreign travel for officials
- **2025 Apr 22** Suspends Chief Justice Torkornoo
- **2025 Jun 10** Joins Global Center on Adaptation board
- **2025 Sep 1** CJ Torkornoo dismissed
- **2026 Feb** Devex Power 50 #5 (Accra Reset)
- **2029 Jan** Constitutional end of term

## First-term achievements (2012–2017)
- Eastern Corridor Road
- E-Block community day SHS programme nationwide
- Free uniforms, exercise books, eliminating "schools under trees"
- University of Ghana Medical Centre Phase 1
- Ridge Hospital expansion
- Bank of Ghana Hospital, Upper West/East/Brong-Ahafo regional hospitals; CHPS
- Atuabo Gas Processing Plant — start of domestic gas-to-power
- Karpowership emergency power & thermal plants
- Kasoa Interchange; KIA Terminal 3 (begun)
- 2015 IMF ECF programme
- Davos 2016: UN SDG Advocate co-chair

## Second-term agenda — "Reset Ghana"
- **24-Hour Economy** — flagship: incentives for 3-shift business operations
- Economic recovery, IMF program, debt restructuring
- National Cathedral special prosecutor investigation
- Lean government — foreign travel restrictions
- Judicial accountability — CJ suspension/dismissal
- Climate adaptation — GCA board
- **Accra Reset agenda** — African-led debt/trade/climate reform

## Pan-African / international

- AU High-Level African Trade Committee Chair (2014)
- UN SDG Advocate co-chair (2016)
